The Challenge

Indoor mmWave networks offer high throughput, but their performance is tightly coupled to the physical scene. Morpheus is built for the regime where layout dynamics, blockage, and human activity make reliability fragile.

Problem Setting

Indoor mmWave networks offer high throughput but are highly sensitive to the physical environment. Small changes such as object movement, human activity, or layout adjustments can disrupt propagation paths and lead to sustained performance degradation.

Existing approaches fail in this regime. Static planning assumes fixed layouts, while reactive mechanisms operate at short timescales and cannot recover from persistent, environment-driven disruptions. In practice, improving performance often requires modifying the physical layout itself.

This creates a constrained search problem. While many layout configurations can improve signal quality, only a small subset are both high-performing and practically feasible under real-world constraints such as safety, usability, and workflow.

Addressing this requires systems that can reason in situ about scene dynamics, anticipate near-future changes, and guide feasible interventions before performance collapses.

Why Reactive Fails

Persistent blockage is a physical-layout problem

Once the environment changes, short-timescale rate adaptation alone cannot restore the lost geometry. The system has to reason about counterfactual layouts, not just RF symptoms.

Core Constraint

Good layouts must also be usable

The best RF solution is not enough. Recommendations must remain feasible for people sharing the space, which makes human feedback a first-class systems signal.

What Morpheus Does

System Summary

Morpheus is a vision-driven wireless digital twin that enables proactive and intent-preserving adaptation in dynamic mmWave environments.

It reconstructs the physical scene from commodity cameras, models RF propagation using a digital twin, and predicts how object-level motion will impact future connectivity. Instead of reacting after degradation, Morpheus surfaces likely blockage events in advance and allows users to explore alternative layouts before applying changes in the real world.

By combining RF-aware evaluation with human feedback, the system identifies configurations that improve performance while remaining practically feasible.

Key Capabilities

01 Predicts near-future object movements that lead to blockage

Models scene dynamics before the network enters a prolonged low-QoE state.

02 Builds RF-aware digital twins from visual data

Connects commodity sensing to a layout-aware representation of the wireless environment.

03 Evaluates counterfactual layouts using ray-traced propagation

Tests alternate object placements before real-world intervention is attempted.

04 Recommends intent-preserving layout adjustments

Optimizes for wireless performance without ignoring how the space must still be used.

05 Incorporates human feedback to enforce real-world constraints

Uses operator input to prune infeasible reconfigurations and steer the search.

Results

Morpheus enables proactive and practical network adaptation under dynamic environments, improving recovery while reducing infeasible interventions.

Throughput Up to 2–3× improvement under blockage

Counterfactual layout evaluation recovers high-rate links that would otherwise remain degraded.

Recovery Speed Order-of-magnitude faster recovery

Proactive guidance reduces the delay of waiting for purely reactive schemes to adapt.

Feasibility 60% fewer infeasible recommendations

Human feedback filters out layout changes that are impractical in shared indoor spaces.

Optimization Faster convergence to intent-preserving layouts

Morpheus narrows the search to configurations that help RF performance without breaking workspace intent.

Why This Matters

Real-World Relevance

Emerging applications such as AR/VR, cloud gaming, and collaborative robotics require stable high-throughput wireless connectivity. However, mmWave performance is tightly coupled with the physical environment, making reliability a persistent challenge in real-world deployments.

Morpheus shifts network management from reactive trial-and-error to proactive, structured adaptation. By reasoning jointly over scene dynamics, RF behavior, and human constraints, it enables practical and scalable debugging of next-generation wireless systems.

Takeaway

From wireless diagnosis to in-situ orchestration

Morpheus positions the digital twin as more than a visualization tool. It becomes a systems interface for anticipating failures, exploring feasible interventions, and closing the loop between physical-space dynamics and network recovery.
